A q-analogue of the hook length formula exhibits cyclic sieving, with evaluations at roots of unity counting the number of rectangular standard Young tableaux fixed by repeated applications of jeu de taquin promotion.

In [combinatorial mathematics](/wiki/Combinatorics "Combinatorics"), **cyclic sieving** is a phenomenon in which an integer [polynomial](/wiki/Polynomial "Polynomial") evaluated at certain [roots of unity](/wiki/Root_of_unity "Root of unity") counts the rotational symmetries of a [finite set](/wiki/Finite_set "Finite set").[\[1\]](#cite_note-1) Given a family of cyclic sieving phenomena, the polynomials give a [_q_\-analogue](/wiki/Q-analog "Q-analog") for the enumeration of the sets, and often arise from an underlying algebraic structure, such as a [representation](/wiki/Representation_theory "Representation theory").

The first study of cyclic sieving was published by Reiner, Stanton and White in 2004.[\[2\]](#cite_note-2) The phenomenon generalizes the "_q_ = −1 phenomenon" of [John Stembridge](/wiki/John_Stembridge "John Stembridge"), which considers evaluations of the polynomial only at the first and second roots of unity (that is, _q_ = 1 and _q_ = −1).[\[3\]](#cite_note-3)

## In representation theory

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Cyclic_sieving&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: In representation theory")\]

The cyclic sieving phenomenon can be naturally stated in the language of representation theory. The group action of C n {\\displaystyle C\_{n}} ![{\displaystyle C_{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0301812adb392070d834ca2df4ed97f1cf132f33) on X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is linearly extended to obtain a representation, and the decomposition of this representation into irreducibles determines the required coefficients of the polynomial f ( q ) {\\displaystyle f(q)} ![{\displaystyle f(q)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7715d0880e2ca04d334d64a3af110b4d1ecdb915) .[\[5\]](#cite_note-5)

Let V \= C ( X ) {\\displaystyle V=\\mathbb {C} (X)} ![{\displaystyle V=\mathbb {C} (X)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9db0d7647f9a91f80699f58c1179c50fdca1efcc) be the [vector space](/wiki/Vector_space "Vector space") over the [complex numbers](/wiki/Complex_number "Complex number") with a [basis](/wiki/Basis_\(mathematics\) "Basis (mathematics)") indexed by a finite set X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) . If the cyclic group C n {\\displaystyle C\_{n}} ![{\displaystyle C_{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0301812adb392070d834ca2df4ed97f1cf132f33) acts on X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) , then linearly extending each action turns V {\\displaystyle V} ![{\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845) into a representation of C n {\\displaystyle C\_{n}} ![{\displaystyle C_{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0301812adb392070d834ca2df4ed97f1cf132f33) .
